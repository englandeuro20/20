England Squad Announcment

Written by EnglandEuro2020 Friday, May 30, 2021

England's final 26-man squad has been announced and as well as some surprise inclusions there are some equally surprising exclusions.

Here is how the team was selected...


Gareth Southgate has named his final squad and ended all speculation of the right-back dilemma, with him opting for all four contested players.

Squad in full:

Goalkeepers (3): Henderson, Johnstone, Pickford

Defenders (10): Alexander-Arnold, Chilwell, Coady, James, Maguire, Mings, Shaw, Stones, Trippier, Walker

Midfielders (5): Bellingham, Henderson, Mount, Phillips, Rice

Forwards (8): Calvert-Lewin, Foden, Grealish, Kane, Rashford, Saka, Sancho, Sterling

As you can see all the usual culprits are included, even Alexander-Arnold who was surrounded by speculation of a call-up due to a shaky middle of the season.

Some more surprising candidates are bellingham and Saka, arguably taking the places of more established Premier league players Lingard and Ward-Prowse. Due to the bigger squad size there were always going to be more debated players both included in the team and excluded.



Players Omitted

The hardest part of naming a tournament side is leaving players at home and in this case there was always going to be 7 men not making the final cut.

Players Left Out:


Ramsdale, Godfrey, Lingard, White, Ward-Prowse, Watkins, Greenwood


Greenwood pulled out of the squad earlier due to injury but whether he would of made the final squad anyway is debatable. It is believed Watkins and Ramsdale were outsiders to get in the team, however the rest of the omissions could have gone either way.
